   - The common practice of prioritizing model size over training time and dataset size might not be optimal for achieving the best model performance (Hoffmann et al., 2022).

### Implications
The findings from these sources imply that scaling large language models effectively requires careful consideration of both model size and training dynamics. Simply increasing model size without corresponding increases in dataset size, compute, and training time may not yield the desired improvements in performance. Moreover, understanding and leveraging the power-law relationships between these factors can help in designing more efficient and effective training strategies for large language models.

### Conclusion
In conclusion, the scaling laws for large language models, as investigated by Kaplan et al. (2020) and Hoffmann et al.
