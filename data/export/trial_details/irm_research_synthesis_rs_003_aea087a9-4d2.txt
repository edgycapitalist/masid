**Synthesis Report: Scaling Laws for Large Language Models**

### Introduction
This report synthesizes findings from two sets of research on scaling laws for large language models. The first set, based on Kaplan et al. (2020) and Hoffmann et al. (2022), establishes power-law relationships between model size, dataset size, compute, and loss, and highlights the potential for undertraining in large models. The second set, based on recent work, suggests that scaling laws may exhibit plateaus for specific capabilities and introduces inference-time compute scaling as a complementary approach to traditional scaling methods.

### Key Findings

1. **Power-Law Scaling Laws**: Kaplan et al. (2020) identified power-law relationships between model size, dataset size, compute, and loss, indicating that:
	* Model size and dataset size are related by a power-law exponent, requiring proportional increases in dataset size to maintain optimal performance.
	* Compute and model size are related by a power-law exponent, suggesting that increasing model size requires a corresponding increase in compute.
	* The loss function decreases as a power-law function of model size, indicating that larger models can achieve better performance.
2. **Undertraining and Optimal Training**: Hoffmann et al. (2022) argued that many large language models are undertrained relative to their parameter count, and that:
	* Increasing training time or dataset size can lead to significant improvements in model performance, even for large models.
	* The optimal training time and dataset size depend on the model size and architecture, suggesting that a one-size-fits-all approach to training may not be effective.
3. **Scaling Law Plateaus**: Recent research suggests that scaling laws may exhibit plateaus for specific capabilities in large language models, where increasing model size or computational resources beyond a certain point yields diminishing returns.
4. **Inference-Time Compute Scaling**: Inference-time compute scaling offers a complementary approach to traditional scaling methods, enabling improved performance through techniques such as chain-of-thought and search.

### Integrated Analysis
The findings from both sets of research can be integrated to provide a comprehensive understanding of scaling laws for large language models. The emergence of unpredictable abilities highlights the complexity of large language models and the need for continued research into their underlying mechanisms.
