The regulation of Artificial Intelligence (AI) is a rapidly evolving field, with countries and regions around the world establishing their own frameworks to govern the development and deployment of AI systems. As AI continues to transform industries and aspects of life, the need for effective regulation has become increasingly important. This synthesis report aims to provide a comprehensive understanding of the current state of AI regulation globally, based on available information.

### Global Approaches to AI Regulation
The European Union has introduced the EU AI Act, which categorizes AI systems into different risk levels (1). This risk-based approach imposes specific requirements on AI systems, with high-risk systems being subject to the most stringent compliance measures. In contrast, China has implemented regulations specifically for generative AI, focusing on content labeling and algorithmic transparency (2). The United States has taken an executive-order approach, prioritizing safety testing over comprehensive legislation (3), while the United Kingdom favors a sector-specific, principles-based framework (4).

### Comparative Analysis
A comparative analysis of these approaches reveals distinct priorities and philosophies. The EU's risk-based approach provides a comprehensive framework for regulating AI systems, while China's focus on generative AI highlights the importance of transparency in specific AI applications. The US emphasis on safety testing suggests a focus on mitigating risks associated with AI, whereas the UK's sector-specific approach implies a recognition of the need for tailored regulation that addresses the unique challenges and opportunities of AI in different contexts. A summary of the key similarities and differences between the four approaches is provided in the table below:

| Region | Approach | Key Features |
| --- | --- | --- |
| EU | Risk-based | Categorization by risk level, strict compliance for high-risk systems |
| China | Focus on generative AI | Content labeling, algorithmic transparency, emphasis on trust and safety |
| US | Executive-order | Focus on safety testing, emphasis on risk mitigation |
| UK | Sector-specific | Principles-based framework, tailored regulation for different sectors |
