**Global AI Regulatory Landscape**
The current state of AI regulation globally is characterized by a mix of risk-based approaches, targeted regulations, and sector-specific frameworks. The European Union's AI Act employs a risk-based approach, categorizing AI systems into four risk levels: unacceptable risk, high risk, limited risk, and low risk [1]. High-risk AI systems, such as those used in healthcare and transportation, are subject to strict compliance requirements, including conformity assessments, human oversight, and transparency obligations. China has implemented regulations specifically targeting generative AI, requiring content labeling and algorithmic transparency [2]. The United States has adopted an executive-order approach, focusing on safety testing rather than comprehensive legislation [3]. The United Kingdom has opted for a sector-specific, principles-based framework for AI regulation, rather than a horizontal regulation approach [4].
