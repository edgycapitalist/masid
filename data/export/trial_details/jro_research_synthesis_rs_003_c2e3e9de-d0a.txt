   - **Model Size**: The number of parameters in the model.
   - **Dataset Size**: The amount of data used for training.
   - **Compute**: The computational resources required for training.
   - **Loss**: The model's performance metric, typically measured as a loss function.

2. **Undertraining Relative to Parameter Count**: The Chinchilla paper by Hoffmann et al. (2022) [2] posits that many large language models are undertrained when considering their parameter count. This undertraining can lead to suboptimal performance, as models may not fully leverage their capacity due to insufficient training data or compute.

- **Training Data Curation**: The insight into undertraining issues can guide the curation of training datasets, focusing on both the scale and relevance of the data to the model's intended application.

- **Compute Resource Planning**: Understanding the computational requirements based on model size and training data size can aid in planning and allocating sufficient compute resources for training large language models.
