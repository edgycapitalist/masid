2. **Model Size and Compute**: The computational resources required to train a model scale with the model size, also following a power-law relationship [1].
3. **Model Size and Loss**: The loss (or error) of the model decreases as the model size increases, adhering to a power-law relationship [1].

### Training Efficiency
Hoffmann et al. (2022), in the Chinchilla paper, posited that most large language models are undertrained relative to their parameter count. This suggests that increasing the amount of training data or the training time can lead to significant improvements in model performance, even for large models [2].

In conclusion, the scaling laws for large language models, as identified by Kaplan et al. (2020), highlight the interdependent relationships between model size, dataset size, compute, and loss. Additionally, the findings of Hoffmann et al. (2022) indicate that many models are undertrained, suggesting opportunities for improvement through increased training data or time.
